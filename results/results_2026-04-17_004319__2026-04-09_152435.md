## Scoring

Judges: panel of LLM-as-judge models — `haiku-4-5` (via Claude CLI) and `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` (via Gemini CLI). Each run's quality score is the mean of both judges, cached per-run so numbers are deterministic across regenerations. Known bias caveats live in the [Judge Consistency Summary](#judge-consistency-summary).

**Tests Quality** = Overall score (1-5) for the generated **test code**.

Dimensions:
- **coverage** — requirements tested
- **rigor** — edge cases + error paths
- **design** — fixture quality + independence
- **overall** — holistic

**Workflow Craft** = Overall score (1-5) for the produced **deliverable** (workflow YAML + scripts, excluding tests).

Dimensions:
- **best_practices** — language-appropriate conventions
- **conciseness** — penalizes dead code AND repetition that should be factored
- **readability** — clarity for a reader encountering it cold
- **maintainability** — modularity, error-handling, testability
- **overall** — holistic

**Duration / Cost** = ratio of each combo's average to the best combo's average on the same axis (lower is better).

Properties:
- **Scale:** ratios, not raw seconds or dollars
- **Band calibration:** auto-calibrated to the data's best-to-worst spread via log-equal division (`boundary_i = max_ratio^(i/12)`), so the best observed ratio lands at A+ and the worst at D-
- **F band:** reserved for ratios beyond the observed worst
## Conclusions

This decision splits into four knobs: model, effort (opus47 only), context length (opus47/sonnet46), and scripting language. Quality figures below are panel means from two LLM-as-judge evaluators (Haiku 4.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro).

- **Model choice:** haiku45 runs $0.45–$0.49 per run at 2.0–2.6 panel-mean quality, a full point below opus47 and sonnet46 on both Tests Quality and Workflow Craft; judge model-ordering agreement is strong (Workflow Craft ρ = +0.94).
- **Effort (opus47 only):** medium → high on opus47-1m adds $0.5–$0.9/run for +0.3–0.8 Tests Quality and Workflow Craft; high → xhigh adds another $0.3–$1.2/run but quality stays flat across most languages.
- **Context length (opus47, sonnet46):** 1m rarely beats 200k at matched effort — sonnet46-200k tops sonnet46-1m on Workflow Craft by ~0.7–0.9 points in most languages, and opus47's 200k/1m split stays within ~0.2 points on most cells.
- **Scripting language:** specifying a language over default moves Tests Quality by up to 0.7 points; default and powershell tie at 4.2 on opus47-1m-high while bash trails at 4.0, and judges disagree sharply on language Tests ordering (ρ = +0.00).

No single configuration leads on cost, speed, Tests Quality, Workflow Craft, and language preference together — weigh these magnitudes against your own priorities.

## Judge Consistency Summary

**🟡 The panel is mostly doing its job, with one bias signal worth tracking:** Both judges put opus47-1m clearly first and haiku45 clearly last on every axis, with strong rank agreement on Workflow Craft (model ρ = +0.94, language×model ρ = +0.76) and decent agreement on Tests Quality model ordering (ρ = +0.66). The flat Tests Quality language correlation (ρ = 0.00) sits inside a 0.35-point cluster where small score noise flips ranks, so it reads as calibration noise rather than real disagreement on which configurations win.

- 👀 **Where to look closer:** Four ⚠️-flagged reversals where Haiku ranks its own haiku45 model above stronger configs Gemini scores much higher — concretely, on 17-artifact-cleanup-script / bash / opus Haiku gives 2 while Gemini gives 5, which leaves bash/haiku45 (mean 2.29) effectively tied with bash/opus (mean 2.14) on Haiku's scale even though Gemini sees a 2.2-point gap.
- 🤓 **Surprise finding:** Haiku prefers sonnet over the older opus on both axes while Gemini prefers opus — the only model-level reversal that persists across both rubrics.
- ℹ️ **Recommended next step:** Hand-grade the four haiku45-vs-opus rows flagged for own-model preference to confirm whether Haiku is genuinely lifting its own rank or just compressing weak outputs against the floor of its 1–5 range.

*Provenance:* `claude-opus-4-7[1m]` at effort `xhigh` via Claude CLI; 5 in / 3045 out tokens, $0.2774. Prompt: [`JUDGE_CONSISTENCY_SUMMARY_SYSTEM_PROMPT`](../judge_consistency_report.py); panel data pooled across all source run directories.
